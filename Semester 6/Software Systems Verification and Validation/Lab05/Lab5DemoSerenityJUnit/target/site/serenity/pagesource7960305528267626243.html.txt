<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" class="mp-contains-float"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 181px;">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag!_World_Tour,_2023_(cropped).jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="SB19"><img alt="SB19" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg/181px-SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="181" height="109" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg/272px-SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg/362px-SB19_on_stage_at_the_Pagtatag%21_World_Tour%2C_2023_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5921" data-file-height="3553"></a></span><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">SB19</div></div>
</div>
<p>"<b><a href="/wiki/Gento_(song)" title="Gento (song)">Gento</a></b>" is a song recorded by the Filipino boy band <a href="/wiki/SB19" title="SB19">SB19</a> <i>(pictured)</i>. It was written by the band's leader, <a href="/wiki/Pablo_(Filipino_musician)" title="Pablo (Filipino musician)">Pablo</a>, and produced along with his brother Joshua Daniel Nase and the record producer Simon Servida. The lyrics of the <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> track are themed around <a href="/wiki/Empowerment" title="Empowerment">empowerment</a> and use <a href="/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold mining</a> as a metaphor for achieving success. <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Philippines" title="Sony Music Philippines">Sony Music Philippines</a> released the song on May&nbsp;19, 2023, as the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from the boy band's second <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP), <i><a href="/wiki/Pagtatag!" title="Pagtatag!">Pagtatag!</a></i> (2023). The song won multiple awards, and critics praised its <a href="/wiki/Catchiness" title="Catchiness">catchiness</a> and lyricism. A dance challenge set to the song became a <a href="/wiki/Viral_phenomenon" title="Viral phenomenon">trend</a> on <a href="/wiki/TikTok" title="TikTok">TikTok</a>. "Gento" achieved top-15 <a href="/wiki/Philippines_Songs" title="Philippines Songs">chart positions in the Philippines</a> and on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> <a href="/wiki/World_Digital_Song_Sales" title="World Digital Song Sales">World Digital Song Sales</a> chart; SB19 became the first Filipino group to enter the chart. The band promoted the song with a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> depicting them mining for gold and with various live performances, including on their <a href="/wiki/Pagtatag!_World_Tour" title="Pagtatag! World Tour">Pagtatag! World Tour</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Gento_(song)" title="Gento (song)">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<h2 id="mp-tfp-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="Today.27s_featured_picture"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Today's_featured_picture">Today's featured picture</span></h2>

<td style="padding:0 0.9em 0 0; width:300px;"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Acorn"><img alt="Acorn" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg/300px-Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="450" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg/450px-Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg/600px-Quercus_robur_acorn_-_Keila.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="6000"></a></span>

<td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Acorn" title="Acorn">acorn</a></b> is the <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nut</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a> tree and its close relatives, in the family <a href="/wiki/Fagaceae" title="Fagaceae">Fagaceae</a>. Acorns usually contain a <a href="/wiki/Seedling" title="Seedling">seedling</a> surrounded by two <a href="/wiki/Cotyledon" title="Cotyledon">cotyledons</a> (seedling leaves), enclosed in a tough <a href="/wiki/Nutshell" title="Nutshell">shell</a> known as the pericarp, and borne in a cup-shaped <a href="/wiki/Calybium_and_cupule" title="Calybium and cupule">cupule</a>. This acorn of the species <i><a href="/wiki/Quercus_robur" title="Quercus robur">Quercus robur</a></i> (the pedunculate oak), with a length of 25 millimetres (1 inch), was photographed in <a href="/wiki/Keila" title="Keila">Keila</a>, Estonia.
